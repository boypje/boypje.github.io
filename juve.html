Interests

I have an interesting perspective on this. My actual degree is in Technology Information, and I’ve done a great deal of both digital electronics and software development in my career. Lately, though, I’ve pretty much been doing power electronics and small signal linear work, mostly because these seem to be areas that younger people either don’t like or don’t understand. It seems that there are way more Computer Engineers coming out of university than there are analog EEs, and they don’t want to deal with any non-digital stuff. Yes, I took the same basic circuit design courses that the other EEs did. I didn’t take any courses in RF electronics or Power for that matter, but I did take the full complement of electronic design. I also did a lot of analog discrete component design as a hobby when I was a kid, so I can do that stuff pretty well. I’d say that it takes a good computer engineer about 1 year to learn to design in one of the analog disciplines. Most of this is learning the current technology - I’d say about 1/2 of design work is component selection. The other half is just the design patterns and testing methodology.
